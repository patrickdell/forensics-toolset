Metadata & Forensic Flags

Extracts EXIF, IPTC, and XMP metadata and checks for forensic indicators: editing software, AI generation tools, timestamp anomalies, and GPS data.

Important: Metadata can be added, removed, or modified without affecting pixel content, and is routinely stripped by social media platforms. Its presence or absence does not prove or disprove authenticity.

Error Level Analysis

ELA re-saves the image as JPEG and measures per-pixel differences. Areas that were edited or composited often show higher error levels than undisturbed regions.

Does not indicate editing: Text, sharp edges, highly-textured regions (fabric, foliage), and repeated saves at the same quality all produce high ELA values naturally.

ELA is one indicator among many and should never be used in isolation.

⚠️ Non-JPEG image. ELA is designed for JPEG. Re-saving introduces compression where none existed — results are shown for reference only and are not meaningful for authenticity analysis.

Clone Detection

Identifies copy-move forgery by finding visually similar regions at different positions. Matched blocks are highlighted with colour-coded overlays.

Limitations: Smooth areas (sky, walls, solid colours) may produce false positives at higher sensitivity. Does not detect AI inpainting or content-aware fill. Start with Low sensitivity.

⚠️ Large image — analysis may take 30–60 seconds.
