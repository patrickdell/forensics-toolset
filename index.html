Metadata Extraction & Forensic Flags

This tool extracts forensically relevant metadata from the image file, including EXIF, IPTC, and XMP data. It checks for signs of editing, AI generation, and timestamp inconsistencies.

Important: Metadata can be added, removed, or modified without affecting pixel content, and is routinely stripped or altered by social media platforms, messaging apps, and image editors. Its presence or absence does not prove or disprove authenticity.

Error Level Analysis (ELA)

ELA detects JPEG compression artifacts by re-saving the image and measuring pixel differences. Areas with high variation may indicate edited or composited regions.

What ELA shows: Areas of re-saved content, local edits, or sharp boundaries.

What ELA does NOT show: Text, sharp graphics, and textured regions (fabric, foliage) naturally show high variation. Repeated saves at the same quality, resizes, and crops also produce variation.

ELA is one indicator among many and should never be used in isolation.

⚠️ Non-JPEG image detected. ELA is designed for JPEG images. Re-saving as JPEG introduces compression where none existed, so high variation appears everywhere. Results shown for reference only.

Clone Detection

Clone (copy-move) detection identifies similar regions at different positions in the image, which may indicate content duplication or forgery.

Limitations: Rotation and scaling are only partially detected. Smooth areas (sky, walls) generate noise especially at high sensitivity. The algorithm does not detect content-aware fill or AI inpainting.

⚠️ Large image. Analysis may take 30–60 seconds.
